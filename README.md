# AI‑Research
This repository serves as a central workspace for ongoing investigations into the **behavior**, **structure**, and **reliability** of modern AI and machine‑learning systems. The focus here is practical, exploratory, and grounded in hands‑on experimentation.

## Overview
The work collected in this project examines several broad areas of interest:

- **Learning behavior and model dynamics**
  - Understanding how different architectures respond to various training conditions, constraints, and problem structures.
    
- **Training stability and failure analysis**
  - Documenting cases where models fail to converge, behave unpredictably, or produce inconsistent results, and exploring the underlying causes.

- **Evaluator and discriminator behavior**
  - Studying how evaluators, critics, or auxiliary networks influence training outcomes, including situations where they introduce drift or misleading signals.

- **Alternative computational approaches**
  - Exploring non‑traditional methods, algorithms, and system designs that differ from standard gradient‑based learning.

- **Entropy, randomness, and system robustness**
  - Investigating techniques for generating, measuring, and maintaining reliable entropy and system health across different environments.

## Purpose

This repository exists to:

- consolidate research notes, experiments, and code

- provide reproducible examples of observed behaviors

- support future exploration into AI system reliability

- share findings openly with the broader community
